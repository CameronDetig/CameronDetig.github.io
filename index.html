Applying for Pixar's Storage Analytics Internship - Summer 2026

CG Production Data Assistant

When I saw Pixar’s posting for the Storage Analytics Internship, it looked like a perfect opportunity to apply the skills I have learned during my MS program. I began planning out a small version of the data assistant system described in the job posting. The concept was quite similar to a project I have worked on for Honeywell so I already had a good idea of how it should work. My plan was to create a portfolio piece for my application, and to also use it as an opportunity to learn more about AWS and containerization.

From working at The Third Floor, I was familiar with the importance of being able to quickly get information on a show or track down a specific file you're looking for. This project seemed like something that would be of real value and be a good application of the LLM / RAG techniques.

The first problem to sort out was what data source I would use for the project. Luckily, Blender Studio offers open source assets from all of their short films going back to 2006. This provided a perfect database that could simulate the sort of production environment that a studio like Pixar would have. Also, using Blender meant it would be easy to install and use in the data processing container, as opposed to a DCC like Maya that would require a license.

About Me

I'm a computer scientist and creative technologist with a unique background bridging the worlds of artificial intelligence and entertainment production. My journey began in film production and digital arts, where I worked as a previsualization artist on major projects for Lucasfilm, Marvel, and Netflix—including Star Wars: Ahsoka. This experience taught me firsthand how critical efficient data management and intuitive tools are to creative workflows.

Currently, I'm pursuing a Master's in Computer Science at UNC Charlotte, specializing in generative AI applications. As a graduate research assistant, I develop LLM-powered systems that transform how users interact with complex databases through natural language. I've built RAG-based chatbots, automated pipeline tools in Python, and systems that make technical data accessible to non-technical users.

I'm excited about the opportunity to contribute to Pixar's Storage and Backup team, where I can combine my technical expertise in AI and data systems with my understanding of production workflows. I believe technology should inspire art, and I'm eager to help build tools that empower artists to focus on storytelling while seamlessly managing petabytes of creative assets.
